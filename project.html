Projects

Completed & Ongoing Projects

The Sustainable E-traceability system for exportable fruits and vegetables of Bangladesh

This project aims to transform the agricultural export sector through the digitization of its entire value chain. By utilizing a mobile app and web-based platform, it establishes a comprehensive database for contract farmers, enabling streamlined transactions and bolstering traceability via QR codes. Moreover, it integrates blockchain technology to enhance supply chain management.

Transformation of Conventional Tea Plantations in Sylhet, Bangladesh

Pilot project to transform conventional tea gardens into organic tea gardens in the northeast of Bangladesh in cooperation with the Bangladesh Tea Board (BTB) and the Ministry of Commerce.
Scheduled project start January 2021.

Organic Cultivation Schemes for Smallholder Cooperatives in Rural Areas of Bangladesh

Development and implementation of organic management plans (OMP) and internal control systems(ICS) for cultivation sites of small holder cooperatives in rural areas of Bangladesh in cooperationwith the project partner Intertrop GmbH, Germany in the framework of the organic certification process in compliance to European regulation (EC) No. 834/2007 in the district Manikganj.
Project start May 2020.

Farmers Training on Organic Farming: Lemubari Organic Farmers Cooperative (LOFAC)

Farmers training on organic farming practices, organic pest control and organic value chain management. Infrastructure supply by IT Alliance Ltd. with farmers training on IT applications by SERA Bangladesh.
Project start October 2019.

Optimization Measures for Garment Production Facilities, Dhaka

Implementation of required quality management in accordance with EU hygiene regulations and work safety, development of management guidelines and optimisation of logistics and production processes at garment production facilities in Dhaka.
Project start June 2019.

Pilot Project on "Organic Jute Cultivation"

Development and establishment of the Pilot Project "Organic Jute Cultivation" to facilitate the integration of jute plants into organic crop rotations, as part of rural development in Bangladesh. This project is conducted in collaboration with the Bangladesh Jute Mills Corporation (BJMC) under the Ministry of Textiles and Jute.
It commenced in February 2019.
